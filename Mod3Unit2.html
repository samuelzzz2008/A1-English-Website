Unit 2: Using Pronouns with Simple Verbs

1. What Are Simple Verbs?

Simple verbs describe basic actions that happen in everyday life. They are easy to understand and form the foundation of English sentences.

VerbMeaning (Indonesian)Example Sentence
eatmakanI eat rice every morning.
gopergiWe go to school every day.
runberlariHe runs fast.
readmembacaShe reads a book.
sleeptidurThey sleep early at night.

2. What Are Pronouns?

Pronouns are words that replace nouns (people, places, or things). Instead of repeating a name, we can use pronouns to make sentences shorter and clearer.

PronounMeaning (Indonesian)Example
ISaya / AkuI am a student.
YouKamu / AndaYou are happy.
HeDia (laki-laki)He eats bread.
SheDia (perempuan)She reads a book.
ItItu (benda/hewan)It runs fast.
WeKami / KitaWe go to school.
TheyMerekaThey sleep early.

3. Combining Pronouns with Verbs

When we combine pronouns and verbs, we create complete and meaningful sentences. Notice how the verb slightly changes depending on the pronoun (especially with “he”, “she”, or “it” — we usually add “s” to the verb).

PronounVerbExample Sentence
IeatI eat breakfast at 7 AM.
YougoYou go to work every day.
HerunsHe runs every morning.
ShereadsShe reads a storybook.
ItjumpsIt jumps on the table.
WeplayWe play football after school.
TheysleepThey sleep at night.

Tip: Always remember — add “s” to verbs after He, She, or It.

Practice saying these sentences aloud. Try replacing the pronouns and verbs to create new ones! Example: “I eat rice” → “He eats rice” → “They eat rice.”
